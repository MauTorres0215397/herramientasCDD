Store Sales - data visualization

In this project we present some visualizations using the pandas, matplotlib and seaborn libraries, using a dataset extracted from Kaggle . This dataset is composed of 5 tables that describe the sales of the "Favorita" store in Ecuador, what type of product was sold, if it was on promotion, the number of transactions, in addition to the oil prices that are suspected to affect the number of sales.
The content of each table is described below.

Dataset content

Train_set: Where sales by product family from January 2013 to September 2017 are described; store_nbr indicates the store number; family, the type of product; sales, the total sales of the family; and onpromotion, the number of products on promotion by family.

stores_set: Describes the metadata of the stores, the city, province, type and its cluster (grouping of similar stores).

oil_set: Describes the daily price of gasoline. Ecuador is a country dependent on the price of gasoline.

holiday_events_set: Describes the holidays and their metadata, which day is celebrated, name, description and if it was transferred or not (the date on which it is celebrated is changed).

transactions_set: Describes the number of daily transactions per store (does not include any additional data)

Data visualization

Sales over Time.

The graph shows the average sales per month at national level, using animation and interpolation we can see how sales change during the 5 years covered by the dataset.
We can also see in yellow the maximum and minimum values.

Percentage of Sales per Family per Year

In the pie chart we can observe the percentage of sales of each family for the year 2014. The slice with the legend other groups all the families with a percentage of less than 2%. We can also see that the largest slice is separated from the rest, to highlight which family sold the most.

Correlation of oil prices and Sales

In order to see if the price of oil affects (either positively or negatively) sales, different correlation diagrams were made, where the average price of gasoline was tied against the average sales per month; by doing this the following observations were obtained.

In the first diagram, as an example, we can observe the data for the year 2014, we can observe a negative correlation, the higher the price of gasoline, the less sales are generated.

Using the data from the 5 years of the dataset (2013-2017) we can observe that the trend of the year 2014 is repeated for all years.

Using a seaborn regplot plot, which uses a linear regression model, we can observe an estimate of the trend of the data. Considering this last graph we can conclude that the correlation between sales and the price of oil is negative, the higher the price of oil, the lower the sales are generated.

Holiday Sales distribution.

Using a boxplot we plot the distributions of sales made on holidays and on normal days. From the graphs we can say that:
The distribution of sales made on holidays is more dispersed than the distribution of sales on ordinary days.
Probably the IQR of the distributions is very similar, to confirm this we would have to do statistical analysis.
The mean of the distributions (represented by the red line) is different

Sales per store

The sales per store are represented in a bar chart, in which, per store number, a bar with the total sales is generated.

In addition to this, a boxplot was made to see which data are outside the IQR, these data were drawn with a different color, yellow for values after Q3 and dark blue for values below Q1.
